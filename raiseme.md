**San Francisco, Summer 2019**

[RaiseMe](https://www.raise.me) was an ed-tech platform where high school students earned micro-scholarships toward college by completing activities like maintaining good grades, participating in community service, and attending AP classes. At the time, 400,000+ students were on the platform with 200+ college partners ranging from Arizona State to Carnegie Mellon. RaiseMe was later acquired by CampusLogic in 2020.

----

### The Role

Business development intern on the Finance team. The internship started with one open-ended prompt: *"Improve product reach through user insights."* From there, I narrowed focus, formulated questions, built context with data, and delivered product recommendations.

### What I Worked On

**Platform Expansion to Community Colleges**
- Remodeled features of the core platform to fit an expansion to community college students — a previously untapped segment

**User Research & Diagnostics**
- Surveyed and interviewed college students who had used RaiseMe in high school to understand drop-off patterns and growth opportunities
- Sampled across regions, age, enrollment date, GPA, test scores, ethnicity, wealth index, and scholarship amount to keep results generalizable
- Used open-ended questions to identify (1) changes in dedication before/after working toward a scholarship, (2) likelihood of signing up for a college version, and (3) reasons behind last-minute drop-offs

**Retention — Observational Study & Modeling**
- Built a study comparing students who extended their RaiseMe membership into college ("Retention") vs. those who dropped off after high school
- Constructed synthetic control groups to produce counterfactual outcomes and derive causal inference
- Developed a model using historical data to assign weights to each activity's impact on reducing dropout likelihood
- Key finding: activities that improved student diligence — attending orientation, not missing classes, campus community engagement — were the highest-impact levers
- Low-income students benefited disproportionately: dropout rates decreased by 8% compared to 3% for higher-income peers

**Predictive Analysis**
- Modeled Summer Melt Withdrawal predictions across 78% of US universities
- Identified the window between admission deposit and enrollment as the highest-impact zone for onboarding

### Outcome

Product recommendations were piloted at Wayne State University and Stetson University. Completed the internship with an "expectations exceeded" result and a full-time return offer.
