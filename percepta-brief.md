# Percepta

---

- **What it is.** Five months old, ~$20M ARR from 5 customers, 35-40 people. One public deployment: Claude chatbot for Maryland food benefits (600K+ served). Claims Fortune 500 and healthcare work but no names beyond Maryland. Targeting the global services outsourcing TAM. Palantir lawsuit cleared but ongoing.

- **Why the team matters.** Palantir SVPs (7-8yr, healthcare/gov), Nevanlinna Prize winner (Daskalakis, proved Nash equilibria are computationally hard), MIT PhDs in game theory + alignment, Berkeley multi-agent RL researchers. This team could have started a lab. They chose enterprise deployment instead. That says something about where they think the bottleneck is.

- **The core bet.** Percepta only works if AI is powerful enough to transform enterprises but still hard enough to deploy that you need specialists. Is deployment complexity structural (HIPAA steps can't be parallelized, FedRAMP has mandatory review periods, even AGI can't make a regulatory cycle run faster than the law allows) or temporary (prompt engineering disappears when the model improves)? The ratio determines everything. Electrification precedent: the motor existed for 40 years before factories redesigned around it. The constraint was never the technology, it was the organization.

- **What's good.** GC distribution (600+ portfolio companies, 20+ health system partnerships, board-level intros). Balance sheet capital with no fund clock. Palantir's FDE playbook learned from the inside. Healthcare focus in the hardest-to-deploy, most defensible vertical. A plausible flywheel where deployment learnings compound across GC's portfolio.

- **How deals happen.** GC board member introduces, McKinsey validates, Percepta deploys. "We're working with McKinsey on this" survives any board question. Portfolio companies are first customers. Own-brand competitive wins against Accenture are 5-10 years away. The thing to watch: whether any of the 5 current customers were won without GC involvement.

- **What worries me.** Scaffolding erodes: 18-month half-life on anything built around current model limitations. Anthropic dependency: building its own FDE team, has pulled model access from competitors before, could shift from selling tokens to selling outcomes. Quality gap (Palantir SVP vs Accenture generalist) probably holds 2-3 years. Percepta's window to build something durable is about the same duration as the gap.

- **The productization question.** 2x clients needs ~2x people right now. Markets assign different multiples to services and software regardless of AI content. Three paths: Mosaic becomes a standalone platform (Palantir Foundry, 15 years), healthcare-specific vertical product (Harvey/Sierra, faster), or proprietary RL models trained on enterprise deployment data (hardest, most defensible if it works). At 35-40 people, doing all three means doing none well.

- **What trajectory.** Medium takeoff has the most probability mass. Healthcare/government/manufacturing adopt slowest. 3-5 year window where deployment expertise matters. 18-36 months to productize: too early and you build wrong abstractions, too late and Accenture+Anthropic ships the platform first. Telecom precedent: 85% of the fiber laid in 1998-2000 was still dark by 2005. Is Percepta building YouTube or laying fiber?

- **What keeps me up.** Whether deployments compound or stay bespoke. Mosaic exists but whether it's a compounding ontology or a deployment toolkit isn't clear from outside. Whether GC's patience is real, because without productization, Percepta's value is its team plus client relationships, both walk out the door. The 3-year quality-gap window, productization window, and Anthropic dependency window all line up. One timeline for everything.

- **How I'd build it.** Institutional integration over technical scaffolding. An hour on HIPAA compliance lasts years, an hour on a prompt chain has an 18-month half-life. Persistent primitives: process discovery methodology, confidence-based routing, compliance frameworks. Each FDE engagement should convert messy workflows into structured agent action spaces that double as RL training data. If deployment generates data, data trains models, models make next deployment easier, then each engagement compounds. Whether that's happening or still aspirational is the question.
