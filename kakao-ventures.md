**Seoul, September 2019 – January 2020**

[Kakao Ventures](https://www.kakao.vc/en) is Korea's most active seed-stage VC, a subsidiary of Kakao Corp with 190+ portfolio companies and $300M+ AUM, investing across deep tech, mobile, digital healthcare, and gaming.

----

### The Role

Venture research intern working on the theme: *"Expanding the VC's network and operations globally, given its strengths in the regional market."* The internship had two distinct workstreams.

### 1. Fellowship Landing Page — A/B Testing

Redesigned the landing page for Kakao Ventures' summer fellowship application to emphasize the prestige associated with the Kakao brand and increase application rates.

- Calculated a required sample of 737 participants per variation (1,474 total) at 80% statistical power and 5% significance level
- Split students randomly across page versions; returning applicants stayed on their original version for consistency
- Ran simulations concurrently to control for web traffic fluctuations, seasonal variation, and other confounds
- Result: **20% increase in fellowship application participation**

### 2. African Venture Ecosystem — Market Research

Conducted a thorough analysis of the African startup and venture capital landscape to evaluate Kakao's potential expansion to the continent.

**Data Collection**
- Compiled a database of the top 50 venture firms operating in Africa
- Scraped firm websites to identify portfolio companies, then used APIs to pull funding round sizes, participant counts, and valuations
- Focused deep analysis on Egypt, Kenya, Nigeria, and South Africa — the markets with the most developed reporting infrastructure

**Market Sizing**
- Used Fermi estimation with sensitivity checks to approximate total VC capital deployed per market, given the lack of centralized reporting platforms comparable to PitchBook or Crunchbase for the region
- Cross-referenced estimates against comparable industry and regional benchmarks

**Stakeholder Interviews**
- Held meetings with Ethiopian, Nigerian, and South African entrepreneurs and investors to ground the quantitative analysis in lived market context
- Navigated personal bias as someone born and raised in Ethiopia by applying impartial evaluation metrics while leveraging insider knowledge to avoid common pitfalls

### Outcome

Delivered a final report and presentation with key findings and recommended entry points to the continent, including detailed market entry strategies per region. Adapted to the Korean office's collaboration style with biweekly check-ins to keep the team aligned throughout.
